#Data_Science_Masters_Dissertation_UEL

The purpose of this study is to develop a ML–augmented gravity framework that integrates the theoretical economic foundations of the structural gravity model with the predictive power of modern supervised learning algorithms. Trailing the work of (Gopinath et al., 2020; Mao, 2025), the study core motivation is to transcend the restrictive linearity assumption of classical gravity estimation, build a flexible, data-driven model that can identify nonlinear interaction effects among GDP, geographic distance, non-tariff barriers, exchange rates, among others, and forecast bilateral trade flows along the Northern Corridor, keeping in view the impact of NTBs. Specifically, the tree-ensemble models including Random Forests, XGBoost (gradient boosting), and neural networks will be applied to predict bilateral trade flows between Kenya, Rwanda and Uganda. 

Core objective of the dissertation
The goal is to build an ML-augmented gravity model of trade for bilateral exports among Kenya, Uganda, and Rwanda (2017–2024 quarterly panel data) to:
1) Predict bilateral trade flows.
2) Quantify the impact of NTBs.
3) Compare traditional gravity estimation against modern machine learning approaches.
4) Maintain interpretability using SHAP.
